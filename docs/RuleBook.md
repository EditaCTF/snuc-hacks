# SNUC Hacks '26 — Rulebook
### Organized by the SNUC Coding Club
#### 25th & 26th March | AB3, SNU Chennai

---

## 1. Overview

SNUC Hacks '26 is the first-ever 24-hour+ Hackathon organized by the
SNUC Coding Club. This event is designed to empower students to solve
real-world problems with innovative, tech-driven solutions. We encourage
creativity, collaboration, and bold ideas.

Open to all SNU & SSN students across all courses.
Team size: 3–4 members.

---

## 3. Hackathon Format

### Structure
- **Problem Statements Released** at 9:30 AM on 25th March
- **Team Leaders Meeting** at 10:00 AM for rules clarification and Q&A
- **Evaluation Rounds** at 3:00 PM (Round 1) and 8:00 AM next day (Round 2)
- **Finals** from 10:00 AM to 2:00 PM on 26th March

### Project Submission Requirements
- A ppt describing the project, key features, and approach
- A working prototype or demo
- Source code hosted on a **public GitHub repository** with regular commits
- A short demo video (3–5 minutes) showcasing the project's functionality

---

## 4. Rules & Regulations

### Venue & Presence
1. Students are not allowed to leave the venue (AB3) during night time. Movement within AB3 (corridors, common areas, open spaces, etc.) is permitted, but participants must not disturb others or cause disruption — **violation will result in disqualification**
2. Participants are free to leave the venue (AB3) during daytime for meals or other needs. All participants must return to the venue by **8:30 PM on 25th March**. If delayed, inform a volunteer in advance along with the reason — **participants who fail to return will be disqualified**
3. At least 1–2 members of the team must be present at their hall at all
   times — **failure to do so may result in a warning or disqualification
   at the organizers' discretion**
4. Participants must carry their ID cards at all times — **those found
   without ID may be asked to leave the venue**
5. Teams are required to be careful not to damage the venue property —
   **teams found responsible for any damage will be held accountable**
6. Organizers are not responsible for loss of personal belongings

### Code & Project
7. All code must be written during the hackathon — no pre-built projects
   — **violation will result in immediate disqualification**
8. Any libraries and tools are allowed
9. No sharing of code or collaboration between teams — **violation will
   result in disqualification of all teams involved**
10. Teams must create a **public GitHub repository** for their project
    and commit regularly throughout the event
11. Teams are required to push their latest commits before every round
    of evaluation — **failure to do so may negatively impact their
    evaluation score**

### Submissions
12. Projects must be submitted before the deadline — no exceptions —
    **late submissions will not be considered for judging**
13. Teams must present/demo their project to the judges — **absence
    during judging will result in disqualification**
14. Every team can only submit up to one project.

### Conduct
15. Respectful behaviour towards all participants, mentors, and
    organizers at all times — **any form of misconduct will result in
    immediate removal from the event**

### Food & Amenities
16. Outside food is allowed
17. Participants will be provided with refreshments during the course
    of the event — no meals such as lunch or dinner will be served
18. Participants are advised to bring toothbrush, paste or other hygiene tools they might require and food.

## 8. FAQs

**Q: Is it online or offline?**
A: Offline! The event will take place in AB3, SNU Chennai.

**Q: What are the timings?**
A: The hack runs from 9:30 AM on 25th March to 8:00 AM on 26th March
(24 hours), followed by evaluation rounds and finals till approximately
2:00 PM on 26th March.

**Q: Do we have to stay for the entire 24 hours?**
A: Participants are expected to remain in the venue for the
duration of the hackathon. Classrooms will be allocated for participants
who wish to rest.

**Q: Can day scholars sleep in AB3?**
A: Yes, classrooms will be allocated for participants to rest in.

**Q: What is the registration fee?**
A: Free! Registration is completely free of charge.

**Q: When does registration close?**
A: Registrations open 18th March and close on 23rd March.

**Q: Are there different tracks?**
A: There are Problem Statements and Open Innovation — no strict tracks.
You're free to expand your solutions beyond the baseline expectations
of the PS as well.

**Q: Will food be provided?**
A: Refreshments will be provided to all participants. However, no meals
such as lunch or dinner will be served — participants are encouraged to
stock up on their own food and snacks.

**Q: Will there be enough power outlets?**
A: We will provide as many extension boards as we can. Teams are
recommended to bring their own extension cords as well.

**Q: Is this open to students outside SNU and SSN?**
A: No, SNUC Hacks '26 is open only to SNU and SSN students.

**Q: What about participants involved in Sports Day on 26th March?**
A: We understand some participants may be involved in Sports Day
activities. Affected teams can have available teammates present for
judging, or coordinate their arrival time with the OC. Priority slots
or post-1:00 PM judging can be arranged if needed — please reach out
to the organizing committee in advance.
